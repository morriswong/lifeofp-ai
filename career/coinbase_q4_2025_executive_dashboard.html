Q4 Financial Highlights
  • Total revenue $1.78B; transaction rev $983M (–6% Q/Q), S&S $727M (–3% Q/Q)
  • Q4 net loss driven by $718M unrealized loss on crypto investment portfolio
  • Adj EBITDA $566M (33% margin); Adj Net Income $178M
  • Q4 total OpEx $1.51B, up 9% Q/Q; SBC $230M (+4% Q/Q)
  • Repurchased 3.3M shares for $850M in Q4; 4.9M more shares for $895M since
FY25 Achievements
  • Total trading volume grew 156% Y/Y to $5.2 trillion; market share doubled to 6.4%
  • 12 products each generating >$100M annualized revenue (half >$250M; 2 >$1B)
  • S&S revenue grew 23% Y/Y to $2.83B — 5.5× vs 2021 cycle peak
  • FY25 net income $1.26B; Adj EBITDA $2.81B (on revenue of $7.18B)
  • Assets on Platform grew 3× in 3 years; Coinbase holds ~12% of all global crypto
Key Risks & Headwinds
  • Consumer take rate compressed by mix shift to Advanced trading and CB1 subscribers
  • Staking revenue declined: lower ETH/SOL prices + falling protocol reward rates
  • Full-time employees +31% Y/Y to 4,951; total OpEx up 35% Y/Y to $5.7B
  • $718M unrealized crypto investment losses in Q4 drove GAAP net loss
  • Q1'26 S&S outlook $550–630M (below Q4's $727M) on lower rates & crypto prices

2025 Product Milestones
  • Closed Deribit acquisition — became global leader in crypto derivatives by open interest and options volume; all-time high revenue in Q4
  • Launched Prediction Markets in the U.S. (powered by Kalshi) — 2 weeks live at time of earnings; appears incremental to existing users
  • Rolled out stock + ETF trading (equities) inside the Coinbase app — 3,000 stocks at launch, ~10,000 by late Feb 2026
  • BTC Borrow originations exceeded $1.5B+ — BTC-backed lending via onchain rails in the Coinbase app
  • Coinbase One Card: ~$800M cumulative spend at end of Q4; ~$3K/month per cardholder
  • U.S. perpetual-style futures 24/7 — first to market; 4× increase in U.S. derivatives market share YoY
  • DEX integration: access to 20M+ assets; expanded to Solana via Jupiter
  • Base reached Stage 1 decentralization (permissionless fault proofs); ~200ms block times; sub-cent fees
Institutional & Regulatory Highlights
  • Custodian for >80% of U.S. BTC and ETH ETF assets; $31B peak ETF inflows in 2025
  • 270+ Crypto-as-a-Service clients; 150 government agencies
  • Largest FCA-registered VASP in the United Kingdom
  • MiCA license secured in Luxembourg; full EEA migration complete
  • India: 2025 was fundamentals (compliance, banking, localization); 2026 focus on revenue growth
  • x402 open standard announced — stablecoin payments for web requests (agentic commerce)
  • Shopify Payments integration: USDC on Base for mainstream commerce
  • Embedded Wallets fully launched for developers; B2B Payments API and UI rolled out

Guidance
🏦 Priority 1: Everything Exchange
  • Single trusted platform for crypto, derivatives, equities, and prediction markets
  • Deepen engagement; help customers manage more of their portfolio where assets reside
  • No material dependence on regulatory unlocks for 2026 execution
  • Prediction markets, equities, and futures already live and growing
💸 Priority 2: Stablecoins & Payments
  • Accelerate USDC utility and payments stack (APIs, wallets, merchant rails)
  • Make value movement faster, cheaper, and always-on for real-world adoption
  • B2B Payments API/UI, Embedded Wallets, Shopify integration already live
  • Disclosure to expand when payments becomes material (no specific TPV guidance yet)
🔗 Priority 3: Bring the World Onchain
  • Expand DeFi integrations in the Coinbase app; scale Base App with focus on trading
  • Continue to grow activity on Base — more usage powered by Coinbase infrastructure
  • Base token under exploration; no update at time of earnings
  • Coinbase Tokenize (Echo + Token Manager) as 2026 growth platform for token issuers
